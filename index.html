About

My work sits at the intersection of mathematical analysis and numerical computation for data-driven dynamical systems. I’m especially interested in reliable spectral information—when it exists—and diagnostics (like residuals and pseudospectra) when it doesn’t.

Practically, I build and test algorithms for kernel-based operator approximation and study failure modes: conditioning, regularization, finite-data effects, and stability of eigendecompositions. I like methods that are both principled and useful.

Research highlights

Residual & pseudospectrum diagnostics for data-driven Koopman approximations

Numerics

I study how residuals and pseudospectra reveal spectral reliability when the operator is non-normal or the spectrum is continuous. This gives a practical “trust meter” for eigenvalues extracted from kernel/EDMD pipelines.

Kernel ridge regression as a lens on Koopman regression & regularization

Theory ↔ Practice

I connect kernel regression views of operator learning with numerical regularization choices (e.g., ridge/Tikhonov) and show how these choices impact stability, prediction, and spectral artifacts.

Scalable GPU implementations for kernel operator pipelines

Systems

Building practical acceleration for large kernel matrices: careful precision choices, multi-GPU parallelism, and stable factorizations/solves. The goal is speed without losing correctness.

Koopman spectral analysis on canonical chaotic systems (e.g., Lorenz)

Applications

Using Lorenz and related systems as “stress tests” for spectral learning: what is identifiable, what is not, and how to quantify error.
